Where Do You Set Up?

Location strategy defines your revenue ceiling, schedule, and lifestyle. A smash burger truck needs consistent, hungry crowds with fast turnover.

Option A
Lunch Rush Circuit
Office parks and business districts, M-F 11am-2pm
Typical Day
1
6:00 AM -- Prep Kitchen
Prep patties, slice toppings, load truck
2
10:30 AM -- Drive to Location
Rotate between 3-4 office park spots weekly
3
11:00 AM -- Service Starts
Peak rush 11:30-1:00, 80% of revenue in 90 min
4
2:00 PM -- Wrap Up
Close service, head back for cleaning
5
3:00 PM -- Done
Clean truck, restock, prep for tomorrow
Pros
  • Predictable weekday schedule
  • Repeat office worker customers
  • Afternoons and weekends free
Cons
  • All revenue in a 3-hour window
  • Vulnerable to remote work trends
  • Permit competition for prime spots

Event Chaser
Festivals, farmers markets, concerts, weekend focus
Typical Weekend
1
Thursday -- Book & Prep

2

3

8am-2pm service, steady all-day traffic
4
Sunday -- Festival/Concert
High volume, long hours, premium pricing
5
Monday -- Recovery
Deep clean, accounting, book next events
Pros
  • Huge single-day revenue potential
  • Built-in crowds, no marketing needed
  • Weekdays mostly free
Cons
  • Unpredictable and seasonal income
  • High event fees ($200-$2000+)
  • Exhausting weekend-only grind

Option C
Brewery Partner

Typical Week

12pm-10pm, brewery events, live music crowds
5
Sunday -- Brunch & Wind Down
11am-6pm, family-friendly afternoon crowd
Pros
  • Guaranteed foot traffic every night
  • No driving or permit hunting
  • Symbiotic: brewery promotes you
Cons
  • Revenue share or rent to brewery
  • Tied to one location's success
  • Limited lunch revenue

Cons

  • Inconsistent traffic by location
  • Residential permit complexities
