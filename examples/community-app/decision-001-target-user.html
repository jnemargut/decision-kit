The Urban Renter
Renter, 25-40, urban apartment or condo
"I need a drill for one shelf. Buying one for $80 that I'll use twice a year makes no sense."
Borrower-First Space-Limited Budget-Aware Tech-Savvy
+ High urgency — real pain point of needing tools they can't store
+ Naturally mobile-first, familiar with sharing economy apps
+ Dense neighborhoods = short travel distance for pickups
- May have fewer tools to lend, creating supply imbalance
- Higher turnover — renters move more often

Maker spaces, woodworkers, renovators
🔨
The DIY Community
Hobbyist or maker, 25-50, active in craft communities
"I'm always looking for a specific router bit or a tile cutter for one-off projects."
Skilled User Niche Tools Community-Driven Project-Based
+ Passionate, engaged users who understand tool value
+ Already organized in communities (forums, maker spaces)
- Niche audience — smaller total addressable market
- Specialty tools need knowledgeable borrowers to avoid damage
Option D
The Eco-Conscious Sharer
Motivated by sustainability and reducing waste
🌱
The Eco-Conscious Sharer
Sustainability advocate, 22-45, values-driven consumer
"Every tool shared is one fewer manufactured. We don't need to own everything."
Values-Driven Community-Minded Early Adopter Low-Waste
+ Strong intrinsic motivation — sharing aligns with identity
+ Vocal advocates who drive word-of-mouth growth
- Sustainability alone may not sustain engagement long-term
- Smaller segment — needs pairing with a functional benefit
