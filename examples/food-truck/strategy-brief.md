# Food Truck Launch Strategy Brief

## SMASH STACK -- Strategy Summary

### The Concept: Smash Burgers
We're launching a smash burger food truck. The thesis is simple: do one thing extraordinarily well. Smash burgers offer the best combination of operational simplicity, low food costs, high throughput, and cult-following potential. The menu is intentionally tight -- Classic Smash ($9), Double Stack ($12), Loaded Fries ($7) -- with seasonal specials to keep regulars engaged.

**Why not the others?** Gourmet tacos have higher ingredient complexity and a saturated market. Asian fusion bowls are gorgeous but slow to serve and expensive to prep. Wood-fired pizza is a spectacle but bottlenecked at 12-18 pies/hour -- not viable for a volume-driven business.

### The Location: Brewery Partnership
Rather than burning fuel and chasing permits, we're partnering with a local brewery that has no kitchen. This is a symbiotic relationship: they need food to keep patrons drinking longer, and we get guaranteed foot traffic without marketing costs.

**The schedule:** Wednesday through Sunday, primarily evenings. Peak revenue on Friday and Saturday nights when the brewery has live music and events. Mondays and Tuesdays off for rest, restocking, and business operations.

**The economics:** Even with a 10-15% revenue share to the brewery, the elimination of driving costs, fuel, and permit fees makes this the highest-margin location strategy. Estimated weekly revenue: $4,500-$7,000.

### The Brand: Neighborhood Joint
"Smash Stack" will feel like your favorite neighborhood spot that happens to be on wheels. Warm tones, hand-lettered typography, and an approachable personality that welcomes everyone from the 25-year-old craft beer enthusiast to the family grabbing dinner.

**Why this vibe?** It's the perfect match for the brewery setting. Street Culture would feel try-hard. Minimal would feel cold. Retro Diner is fun but forgettable. The Neighborhood Joint aesthetic builds the kind of local loyalty that turns first-time visitors into weekly regulars.

## Decision Trail

1. **Concept:** Smash Burgers -- chosen for operational simplicity, low costs, high throughput, and cult-following potential
2. **Location:** Brewery Partner -- chosen for guaranteed traffic, low overhead, and symbiotic marketing
3. **Brand:** Neighborhood Joint -- chosen for warmth, broad appeal, and brewery-setting fit
