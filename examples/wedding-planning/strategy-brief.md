# Wedding Planning Strategy Brief

## Vision

A romantic, conversation-driven celebration set in a garden estate, captured through honest documentary photography. Every decision reinforces one core idea: **this wedding is about the people, not the production.**

---

## Decision Summary

### 1. Venue Style: Garden Estate
**Why:** The garden estate scores highest across ambiance, guest comfort, and photo opportunities while keeping weather risk manageable with a tented reception. It provides a timeless backdrop that won't feel dated in photos twenty years from now. The manicured grounds offer endless locations for portraits without requiring the couple to leave the property.

**What this enables:** Long communal tables under the tent, natural light for photography, outdoor ceremony with indoor backup, and a setting that does much of the decorating on its own.

### 2. Guest Experience: Family Style
**Why:** Family-style dining at long communal tables creates the warm, inclusive atmosphere the couple wants. Shared platters encourage conversation between guests who might not otherwise meet. It pairs perfectly with the garden estate setting, where long tables under a tent with string lights create an almost Tuscan dinner-party atmosphere.

**What this enables:** Natural interaction between different friend groups and families, beautiful tablescapes that photograph well, a relaxed pace that lets toasts happen organically, and a lower per-plate cost than plated service.

### 3. Photography: Documentary Style
**Why:** Documentary photography captures the real emotions of the day rather than manufactured moments. It lets the couple stay present and enjoy their wedding rather than spending hours posing. The family-style dinner and garden setting will produce naturally photogenic moments that a skilled documentary photographer will capture effortlessly.

**What this enables:** More time with guests instead of in photo sessions, images that feel authentic and emotional when revisited, faster turnaround time, and a lower cost that can be redirected to other priorities.

---

## How the Decisions Connect

These three choices create a coherent wedding identity:

- **Garden Estate + Family Style** = Guests seated at long tables on beautiful grounds, natural light streaming through the tent, shared food creating a communal warmth
- **Family Style + Documentary Photography** = Candid shots of guests laughing, passing platters, clinking glasses - the kind of moments that look better unposed
- **Documentary Photography + Garden Estate** = A photographer who can roam the grounds capturing real moments against a naturally stunning backdrop

The thread connecting all three decisions: **authenticity over performance.** The venue is naturally beautiful rather than over-decorated. The dining is shared rather than served. The photography is observed rather than directed.

---

## Key Trade-offs Accepted

- **Tent rental adds cost** - but provides weather insurance and beautiful atmosphere
- **Fewer formal posed portraits** - but the real moments will mean more over time
- **Dietary accommodations need advance planning** - but the variety of shared platters makes this manageable
- **Seasonal availability constraints** - early booking required to secure peak garden season

---

## Next Steps

1. Research and visit 3-5 garden estate venues within the target area
2. Book venue with tent rental package for preferred date
3. Source family-style caterers who specialize in seasonal, shareable menus
4. Interview documentary wedding photographers and review full wedding galleries
5. Begin coordinating vendors around the chosen venue's layout and timing
